<p class="aboutElementDetailShort">Name: <span class="aboutElementDetailShortNum">Magnesium</span></p>
<p class="aboutElementDetailShort">Symbol: <span class="aboutElementDetailShortNum">Mg</span></p>
<p class="aboutElementDetailShort">Atomic Number: <span class="aboutElementDetailShortNum">12</span></p>
<p class="aboutElementDetailShort">Atomic Mass: <span class="aboutElementDetailShortNum">24.305</span></p>
<p class="aboutElementDetailShort">Group: <span class="aboutElementDetailShortNum">Alkaline Earth Metal</span></p>

<p class="aboutElementDetailParagraph">Magnesium, an essential alkaline earth metal, plays a vital role in various biological processes and industrial applications. Represented by the symbol Mg and possessing atomic number 12, magnesium is characterized by its lightness, reactivity, and wide-ranging applications.</p>

<p class="aboutElementDetailParagraph">Physically, magnesium is a shiny gray solid and is notable for being one of the lightest structural metals. Its low density and high strength-to-weight ratio make it a valuable component in industries requiring lightweight and durable materials.</p>

<p class="aboutElementDetailParagraph">One of the key applications of magnesium is in the aerospace industry, where its combination of strength and low weight contributes to the construction of aircraft and spacecraft components. Magnesium alloys are also used in the automotive industry to reduce the weight of vehicles, improving fuel efficiency.</p>

<p class="aboutElementDetailParagraph">Biologically, magnesium is an essential element, playing a crucial role in various cellular processes. It is a cofactor for many enzymes, involved in energy production, nucleic acid synthesis, and muscle function. Magnesium is also a component of chlorophyll, contributing to photosynthesis in plants.</p>

<p class="aboutElementDetailParagraph">In industry, magnesium compounds find applications in the production of refractory materials and as a reducing agent in the extraction of metals. Magnesium oxide is used in the production of refractory bricks, while magnesium is a key element in the production of titanium, zirconium, and other metals.</p>

<p class="aboutElementDetailParagraph">Isotopes of magnesium include magnesium-24, magnesium-25, and magnesium-26. Magnesium-24, being the most abundant isotope, is used in nuclear reactors as a neutron absorber and moderator.</p>

<p class="aboutElementDetailParagraph">The historical significance of magnesium dates back to its discovery by Sir Humphry Davy in 1808. Davy isolated magnesium through the electrolysis of magnesium oxide, marking a milestone in the exploration of alkali earth metals.</p>

<p class="aboutElementDetailParagraph">As we continue to unlock the potential of materials science and delve into the intricacies of biological systems, magnesium remains a versatile and indispensable element. Its unique combination of properties positions it as a critical player in advancing technology, industry, and our understanding of life processes.</p>
